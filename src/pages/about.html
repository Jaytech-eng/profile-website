ABOUT ME

I’m Okoro Johnson, a multidisciplinary professional working at the intersection of technology, academia, business strategy, and consulting. My work is driven by a simple belief: when knowledge is well-structured and systems are well-designed, people perform better and organizations grow sustainably. With a foundation in Library and Information Science, I have built, managed, and supported information systems in academic settings, including contributing to the establishment of a school library from the ground up and serving as a teacher-librarian.

These experiences shaped my analytical thinking, research orientation, and deep appreciation for organized knowledge and digital information access. My career has expanded into business and operations management, where I currently lead retail operations within a high-volume commercial environment. This role sharpened my skills in process optimization, leadership, customer experience, and data-informed decision-making. To complement this experience, I am pursuing a Master’s degree in Business Administration (MBA), with a focus on strategic thinking and organizational growth.

In the tech space, I work as a tech consultant and digital support professional, helping individuals and small businesses navigate technology with clarity—especially beginners transitioning into tech. I also provide virtual assistant and operational support services, bridging the gap between digital tools and business efficiency. As an entrepreneur, I have launched and managed brands across fashion and technology retail, strengthening my hands-on understanding of branding, systems, and market positioning. Across all these fields, my strength lies in connecting information, people, and processes—turning complexity into structure and ideas into actionable systems.
